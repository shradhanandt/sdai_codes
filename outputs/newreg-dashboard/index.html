Leadership Narrative

📋 Executive Storyboard — March 2026 New Registrations

1

The Business is Growing at Scale

In the first 17 days of March 2026, 320,812 new non-premium domains were registered across our 12-TLD portfolio — a daily run rate of ~18,871. If sustained, this projects to over 566,000 registrations for the full month. The numbers reflect a healthy, active marketplace and strong partner distribution.

2

Three TLDs Carry the Portfolio — Six Others Need Investment

.online (36.9%), .site (20.9%), and .store (20.4%) account for 78.2% of registrations. Meanwhile .fun (5.0%), .website (2.3%), .in.net (0.85%), .pw (0.54%), .uno (0.25%), .host (0.13%), and .press (0.10%) collectively contribute just 9.1%. These TLDs represent untapped diversification — targeted promotions and registrar incentives can shift the mix without cannibalising existing volume.

3

Namecheap and Hostinger Lead — REG.RU and Dotserve Are Rising

Namecheap (67,376) and Hostinger (47,545) represent 35.8% of volume. Hostinger leads .online with 20,208 registrations, overtaking GoDaddy (13,067) on that TLD. REG.RU (14,810) signals strong Eastern European market traction. Dotserve Inc (14,166) dominates .tech at 32.6% share — a specialist channel worth strategic deepening. GoDaddy at 9.9% overall remains critical but warrants co-marketing review.

4

98% 1-Year Registrations Signal a Renewal Cliff Ahead

With 315,504 of 320,812 registrations (98.3%) being 1-year contracts, there is a large renewal event coming in Q1 2027. This is both a risk and an opportunity. Renewal rates for non-premium domains average 50–65% industry-wide. Implementing renewal nudge campaigns, multi-year discount offers, and registrar co-incentives now could materially improve the renewal conversion rate and lock in ARR.

5

166 Registrar Partners — A Long Tail Worth Nurturing

Beyond the top 5 registrars (who hold 62% of volume), there are 161 additional registrar partners contributing 38% of registrations combined. Many of these may be regional players with strong local market penetration. An enablement program for mid-tier registrars — training, co-marketing, tooling — could unlock significant untapped volume without requiring new partner recruitment.

6

Recommended Actions

① Diversify TLD promotion — increase spend behind .fun, .website, .in.net, and .uno; reduce concentration risk in top 3 TLDs.
② Partner health review — investigate GoDaddy relative share decline on .online; deepen co-marketing with Hostinger and Dotserve given their growth.
③ Renewal preparedness — begin Q1 2027 renewal strategy now; target multi-year upsell at point of registration.
④ Emerging market focus — .in.net (India) and .uno (Spanish-language) are early-stage; assign dedicated regional registrar partners to accelerate adoption.
